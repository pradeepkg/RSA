Canvas

January 27, 2024 · 44 min read
Pradeep Kadambar
Creator

Canvas LMS is a versatile Learning Management System known for its user-friendly interface and robust features. Widely used in educational institutions, it facilitates seamless course management, collaboration, and assessment. With customizable dashboards and mobile accessibility, Canvas enhances the teaching and learning experience, making it a preferred choice for educators and students alike.

Identities ❌ | Accounts ✔️ | Groups ✔️ | App Roles ✔️ | Entitlements ❌


Versions: 8.0.0 P1

Overview

In this section, we delve into various Canvas objects that hold significance from an access management standpoint. The insights gained here will be used in later sections of the blog, where we explore the configuration of data collection within G&L.

Account-Level Roles

  • Users at the Account Level: Users are created at the account level, representing individuals within the entire Canvas LMS system.
  • Account-Level Roles: Different roles are assigned to users at the account level.
    • Administrator: Manages system settings, users, and courses at the institutional level.
    • Account Manager: Oversees account-level settings and user management.
    • Support: Assists users with technical support.

Course-Level Roles

  • Users at the Course Level: Users are enrolled in specific courses within the Canvas LMS. These users may include teachers, students, teaching assistants (TAs), and other roles.
  • Course-Level Roles: Different roles are assigned to users at the course level.
    • Teacher: Manages and instructs a specific course, with permissions for content creation, grading, and communication.
    • Student: Enrolls in and participates in courses, with access to assignments, discussions, and materials.
    • TA (Teaching Assistant): Assists teachers with course management and grading.

Relationship Between Users and Roles

  • At both the account and course levels, users are assigned roles to define their access and permissions within the system.
  • Users can have different roles at the account and course levels simultaneously. For example, an individual might be an administrator at the account level and a teacher in specific courses.

Permissions and Responsibilities Roles dictate the permissions and responsibilities of users. For instance, a Teacher at the course level can manage content, grade assignments, and interact with students within the confines of that course, while an Administrator at the account level has broader control over system-wide settings and user management.

Account Collector

This section explains the process for configuring an account collector for the Canvas application. We will use the RSA G&L Generic REST collector for this purpose.

Steps

  1. Login to RSA G&L console as System Administrator.
  2. Navigate to Resources > Applications.
  3. Click on Canvas.
  4. Navigate to Collectors tab and click on Create Account Collector. Enter the details as shown below.

  5. Click Next and enter the connection details. This article will use Token based authentication to invoke REST APIs.

    💡Tip

    Canvas APIs do not support data filtering and hence collections may sometimes return a 504 Gateway Time-out error. A suitable time out settings in the collector might be needed.

  6. Click Next. Select the data types that the collector will be collecting from Canvas

  7. Click Next. On the Account Data page, enter the following configurations.




    Request
    MethodGET
    URL/api/v1/accounts/self/users
    Request Headers
    Acceptapplication/json
    AuthorizationBearer ${AuthenticationToken}
    Response Mappings
    AccountId$.*.login_id
    Id$.*.id
    SISId$.*.sis_user_id
  8. Click Next. On the Group Data page, enter the following configurations.




    Request
    MethodGET
    URL/api/v1/users/self/groups
    Request Headers
    Acceptapplication/json
    AuthorizationBearer ${AuthenticationToken}
    Response Mappings
    GroupId$.*.name
    Id$.*.id
    Description$.*.description
  9. Click Next. On the Account Group Membership page, enter the following configurations.




    Request
    MethodGET
    URLapi/v1/groups/${Id}/users
    Request Headers
    Acceptapplication/json
    AuthorizationBearer ${AuthenticationToken}
    Response Mappings
    Account Identifier$.*.id
  10. Click Next. In the Map Collector Attributes to Account Attributes, map the collected account attributes.

  11. Click Next. In the Map Collector Attributes to Account Mapping Attributes, map the user reference attribute.

  12. Click Next. In the Map Collector Attributes to Group Attributes, map the collected group attributes.

  13. Click Next. In the User Resolution Rules, map the user to account.

  14. Click Next. In the Member Account Resolution Rules, map the account attribute.

    info

    This value MUST always be mapped to the Account Name for any generic REST collector.

  15. Click Finish. Use the Test function to make sure the configurations are accurate.

Entitlement Collector

This section explains the process for configuring an entitlement collector to collect roles from Canvas. We will use the RSA G&L Generic REST collector for this purpose.

Steps

  1. Login to RSA G&L console as AveksaAdmin.
  2. Navigate to Resources > Applications.
  3. Click on Canvas.
  4. Navigate to Collectors tab and click on Create Entitlement Collector. Enter the details as shown below.

  5. Click Next and enter the connection details as shown in Step 5 of Account Collector.
  6. Click Next. Select the data types that the collector will be collecting from Canvas.

  7. Click Next. On the App Role Data page, enter the following configurations.




    Request
    MethodGET
    URL/api/v1/accounts/self/roles
    Request Headers
    Acceptapplication/json
    AuthorizationBearer ${AuthenticationToken}
    Response Mappings
    ApplicationRoleId$.*.role
    Id$.*.id
  8. Click Next. On the App-Role Account Membership collection page, enter the following configurations.




    Request
    MethodGET
    URL/api/v1/accounts/self/admins
    Request Headers
    Acceptapplication/json
    Authorization${AuthenticationToken}
    Response Mappings
    App-Role Identifier$.*.role_id
    Account Identifier$.*.user.id
  9. Click Next. In the Map Collector Attributes to App Role Attributes, map the collected account attributes.

  10. Click Next. In the Account Evaluation, map the account collector to account value.

  11. Click Next and then click Finish. Use the Test function to make sure the configurations are accurate

Application Setup

This section explains the process for setting up application for API access with G&L.

Steps

  1. Login to Canvas console with administrative access.
  2. Navigate to Setting https://instance.instructure.com/profile/settings.
  3. Under Approved Integrations, click on New Access Token.
  4. Specify the Purpose and leave the Expires blank. Click Generate Token.

  5. Copy the token for use with G&L.
